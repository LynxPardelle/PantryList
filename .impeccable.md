## Design Context

### Users
PantryList is for people managing a real home inventory: food, hygiene, and
cleaning products shared across daily routines. Users want to reduce waste,
know what should be used first, and avoid buying duplicates without turning
household management into a complex operations job.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel like a "hogar operativo": practical, calm, and
configurable. It should combine the clarity of a household helper with enough
control for users who want reliable inventory rules, expiration thresholds, and
planning behavior.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a warm, clear, domestic interface with operational depth. The UI should be
light by default, readable in quick household moments, and structured enough to
support detailed configuration without becoming a corporate admin dashboard.

Avoid these directions:
- Corporate SaaS dashboards that feel cold, generic, or enterprise-heavy.
- Childlike/playful apps with excessive illustrations or game-like styling.
- Medical or alarm-heavy interfaces that make expiration warnings feel anxious.

### Design Principles
- Make the next household action obvious: use, review, restock, or configure.
- Keep alerts visible but calm; expired products need priority without panic.
- Reveal advanced controls progressively so casual users are not overloaded.
- Treat configuration as a guided control panel, not a pile of switches.
- Prefer warm structure, strong hierarchy, and concise copy over decorative UI.
